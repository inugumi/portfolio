Maynooth Furniture

I managed the UX/UI design for Maynooth, a fictitious furniture startup tailored toward higher-end design furniture and styles. I conducted research and user testing. I produced sketches, wireframes, and mockups. The primary goal was to create a site that was highly visual and allowed users to determine the latest trends and styles.

This was a process that was taken from start to finish so that I could gain a better understanding of the general UX design process.

Sketches

Rapid sketches for both web and mobile were created. Feedback was taken from professors, friends, and students. Changes were made accordingly and a second iteration of sketches were created.

Wireframes

Focused on wireframing high impact pages to gain a better foundation for the direction the design would take. The first iteration of low res wireframes ended up being reworked into a layout more suitable for the website based on a series of user testing and feedback from peers.

Mockups

Once there was a solid design direction, I produced high fidelity mockups of both web and mobile that could then be converted into prototypes. This provided the best case for user testing, as there was no longer any ambiguity to the design.
